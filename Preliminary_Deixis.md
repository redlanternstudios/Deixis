# PRELIMINARY DEIXIS — Brand Reference

> **Brand essence:** Spatial ambiguity. Frames-within-frames. The gallery as a linguistic act.

---

## 1. Etymology & Positioning

**Deixis** (pronounced *DYEK-sis* / *DAYK-sis*) is a linguistic term — words like *here, now, this, that* — whose meaning depends entirely on context. A deictic word points, but what it points to shifts with the speaker, the moment, the frame.

For a digital gallery, this is the conceptual foundation: every artwork exists in a context. The frame, the wall, the lighting, the arrangement — all of it *points* to meaning that is never fixed. The viewer completes the sentence.

**Tagline concepts:**
- *Point of View*
- *Here. Now. This.*
- *Framing the Context*
- *Every Artwork Points Somewhere*

---

## 2. Brand Personality

| Trait | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Curatorial | Selects, frames, presents — never overwhelms |
| Precise | Clean lines, intentional spacing, no clutter |
| Warm | Not cold-minimalist — galleries should feel human |
| Contextual | The UI disappears; the art and its framing remain |
| Contemporary | Current, not trendy. Built for the 2020s, not chasing them |

## 3. Color Direction (Preliminary)

These are directional — to be locked during Phase 1 design:

| Role | Direction | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Background | Warm off-white (#F7F5F0 approx) | Gallery-wall feel, not sterile white |
| Surface | Pure white (#FFFFFF) | Cards, modals |
| Text Primary | Near-black (#1A1A1A) | High contrast, readable |
| Text Secondary | Muted graphite (#6B6B6B) | Captions, metadata |
| Accent | Warm gold / ochre (#C9A84C) | Links, highlights, active states |
| Accent Alt | Muted terracotta (#B8736A) | Secondary highlights, tags |
| Border | Light warm gray (#E5E0D8) | Dividers, card borders |

## 4. Typography

- **Primary:** Geist Sans (system font via Next.js)
- **Detail/Mono:** Geist Mono
- **Fallback stack:** `-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, sans-serif`

---

## 5. Spatial Principles

The gallery metaphor extends to spacing:
- Generous whitespace — art needs room to breathe
- Centered layouts, not full-bleed
- Subtle shadows for card elevation (not flat, not floating)
- Grid as gallery wall: uniform or asymmetric depending on collection

---

## 6. Voice & Tone

- **Descriptive** — explain the work, don't sell it
- **Quiet** — let the art speak first
- **Precise** — exact dimensions, materials, dates
- **Inviting** — "explore" not "browse"
